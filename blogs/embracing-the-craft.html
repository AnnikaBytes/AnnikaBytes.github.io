Embracing the Craft

Building my first personal website has been more than just an exercise in HTML and CSS. It’s been an exploration of digital identity and the quiet satisfaction of finding beauty in the details. In a world where templates dominate, there’s something profoundly rewarding about hand-coding a space that feels uniquely your own.

As a software engineering student at Cal Poly, I spend a lot of time thinking about functionality and logic. But this project reminded me that software is also a craft. The way a serif font interacts with an elegant script, the precise amount of white space between sections, the subtle transition of a link on hover—these details matter. They transform a collection of tags into an experience.

The Editorial Aesthetic

I wanted this site to feel like a high-end magazine or a carefully curated scrapbook. I gravitated towards a monochrome palette—not because of a lack of color, but because of the clarity it brings. Black and white, when used with intent, highlights the structure and the typography, allowing the content to speak for itself.

The journey of a thousand miles begins with a single commit. This site is just the beginning, and I’m excited to see how it evolves as I continue to learn and grow in this field. Thank you for being part of the journey.
